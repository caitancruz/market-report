Market Restraints: Challenges Hindering Growth
High Production Costs
The production of high-quality chiffon involves intricate weaving processes and the use of delicate fibers, leading to higher costs compared to synthetic alternatives. This can limit market growth in price-sensitive regions.
Durability Issues
Chiffon fabric is prone to tearing and fraying, making it less durable than other textiles. This factor can discourage consumers from frequent purchases, particularly for daily wear garments.
