Despite its growing popularity, cupping therapy faces skepticism due to the limited clinical evidence supporting its long-term efficacy. The absence of standard treatment guidelines poses a challenge for regulatory approval and wider adoption.

Cases of skin burns, bruising, and infections associated with improper cupping techniques have raised concerns among healthcare professionals. This may hinder the widespread acceptance of cupping therapy, particularly among first-time users.
